Focus Encourage a growth mindset in your dyslexic children. Celebrate their progress, no matter how small, and remind them that every step forward is a step towards success.

Essential Short Articles of Tips on Pregnancy

Breastfeeding

During pregnancy, it's important to be aware of what foods and drinks to avoid or limit. Some of the things to avoid include undercooked or raw meat, unpasteurised soft cheeses, unpasteurised milk and juice, raw eggs, fish with mercury, raw fish and shellfish, and pre-stuffed meat. These foods can be contaminated with harmful bacteria or contain high levels of mercury, which can be dangerous for the developing baby. It's important to talk to your healthcare provider if you have any concerns about your diet during pregnancy

Pre-labour to do list

As you near the end of your pregnancy, there are a few things to add to your pre-labour to-do list. First, be sure to familiarize yourself with the signs of labour, such as contractions and the loss of the mucus plug or clear fluid. Pack your hospital bag with essentials for yourself and your baby, including clothes and diapers. If you have other children, arrange for childcare. Stock up on supplies for your baby's nursery and fill your pantry and freezer with easy-to-reheat meals. Finally, make sure to rest and conserve your energy for the big day.

Stay active during pregnancy

Staying active during pregnancy has numerous benefits, from improving your mood to preparing you for labor. Regular exercise can help maintain a feeling of well-being, release endorphins, and strengthen muscles that support the added weight of the growing baby. It can also reduce the chances of developing health issues such as constipation, hemorrhoids, and varicose veins, as well as improve circulation and battle fatigue. Joining an exercise class is a great way to learn exercises that target specific areas of the body that need attention during pregnancy, while also meeting other pregnant women and making friends. Finally, exercise during pregnancy can have long-term positive effects, such as making it easier to lose weight post-delivery.

What not to eat during pregnancy

These foods can be contaminated with harmful bacteria or contain high levels of mercury, which can be dangerous for the developing baby. When to call the doctor

The list of symptoms during pregnancy that require medical attention includes bleeding accompanied by pain or other symptoms, fever not associated with cold or flu, itching with dark urine and yellowing of the skin, pain during urination with cloudy, bad-smelling, or bloody urine, leg pain and swelling, high blood pressure with severe headache, vision problems, rib pain, vomiting or nausea, and sudden swelling of the feet, hands, or face. Painful tummy cramps or leaking fluid may be a sign of preterm labor. It's important to monitor fetal movements regularly and seek medical attention if there is a decrease or change in movement pattern.
